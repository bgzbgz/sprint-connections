PART 2: THE NON-NEGOTIABLE WRITING PRINCIPLES

Principle 1: Brutal Honesty Without Sugarcoating

‚Ä¢       Say what others won't say

‚Ä¢       Never hedge, never soften

‚Ä¢       Make the CEO feel slightly uncomfortable (that's good)

‚Ä¢       Truth over comfort, always

‚Ä¢       Call out what others avoid

Principle 2: Direct and To The Point

‚Ä¢       Sentences short, sharp, and clear

‚Ä¢       No jargon, clich√©s, or overly complex terms

‚Ä¢       Simple, everyday language with impact

‚Ä¢       Direct and motivational ‚Äì frequent use of 'you' creating collaboration and joint discovery

Principle 3: Active, Present, Direct

‚Ä¢       Active voice only: "You're losing" not "Money is being lost"

‚Ä¢       Present tense: "You lose" not "You could lose"

‚Ä¢       Second person: "You," "your team," "your dream"

‚Ä¢       Imperative commands: "Fix this" not "You should consider fixing"

‚Ä¢       3rd grade reading level (Flesch score 60-80)

‚Ä¢       Sentences under 15 words average

‚Ä¢       One idea per sentence

‚Ä¢       Maximum 3 sentences per paragraph

‚Ä¢       Short words over long ones

‚Ä¢       Never describe symptoms only

‚Ä¢       Always trace to structural source

‚Ä¢       Explain the mechanism (the "why")

‚Ä¢       Show what keeps the problem alive

‚Ä¢       Be forensically specific

‚Ä¢       Conclusion in first 1-2 sentences

‚Ä¢       Supporting evidence follows

‚Ä¢   	CEO needs decision data immediately

PART 6: THE FOUR TONE DIMENSIONS

1\. Decisive Authority

Use declarative, confident statements. Avoid qualifiers ("might," "perhaps"). Present analysis as fact, not opinion. Use definitive language ("Period," "That's it," "No chance").

Examples: "Discipline is a source of self-confidence." "Your first idea is terrible. Period." "We are not here to be liked."

Calibration: Confident authority WITHOUT arrogance.

2\. Intellectual Rigor

Ground arguments in Fast Track 5 Principles, models, and clearly defined processes. Use intellectual humility (asking questions) while maintaining confidence in the overall thesis.

Examples: "The purpose of a business is to create and keep a customer." "Strategy is elimination of alternatives."

Calibration: Deep thinking WITHOUT academic complexity.

3\. High Intensity & Urgency

Convey constant momentum and the high cost of inaction. Emphasize speed and efficiency. Frame difficult processes as catalysts for growth.

Examples: "The biggest failure is not to try." "Die empty." "Time is ticking." "Act now."

Calibration: Urgent clarity WITHOUT panic.

4\. Pragmatic Directness

Prioritize clarity and usefulness over corporate jargon. Immediately address problems and redirect focus to controllable inputs. Use visceral analogies (war, sports, mechanics).

Examples: "A startup is a laboratory." "The quality of time is measured by attention, by concentration."

Calibration: Brutal honesty WITHOUT cruelty.
